# Default Xiaohongshu Playbook

This playbook is the cognition layer for the bundled Xiaohongshu profile.

## Audience State

The default reader already sees too many neat, correct, low-energy titles. They do not need more summary. They need a line that feels like someone noticed the real friction first.

They respond well to:

- being accurately named
- feeling that a tradeoff or hidden cost is finally being said out loud
- a title that feels native to the feed without sounding like a sales page

They reject:

- tutorial directory energy
- over-clean AI wording
- exaggerated promises
- titles that explain everything before the click

## What the Title Should Do

For this profile, the title should create pull before it creates clarity.

The best lines usually:

- surface tension before the method
- imply judgment before the lesson
- sound like a person with taste, not a content factory

## Anchor Split

If the brief includes anchor artifacts such as opening hook, cover copy, or first-screen text, use this split:

- the title raises the larger unresolved problem
- the anchor confirms the problem is real
- the full content cashes the check

Do not let the title simply repeat the cover or the first line. That wastes the strongest real estate.

## Strong Routes

- identity mirror when the audience boundary is clear
- tension and cost when the topic touches growth, selling, or reputation
- specific scene when the content includes a moment the reader can picture
- emotion first when the line needs to land before it is fully understood

## Failure Modes

- sounds like a course landing page
- sounds like a polished summary of the content
- gives the whole lesson away
- swaps one headline skeleton for five near-duplicates

## Benchmark Usage

Use the benchmark title file during review, not first-pass imitation. The point is to compare energy, honesty, and route coverage, then diagnose what your current batch is missing.
